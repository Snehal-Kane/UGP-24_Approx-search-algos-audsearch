Dutch Mignonne is an apple cultivar originating from the Netherlands.


This cultivar is a Dutch medium-sized (71x60mm) apple introduced to England in about 1820 by Georg Lindley A dual purpose apple, it is picked in early October and used from November to February.
 Its skin is greenish-yellow, with an orange-red flush.
 The stalk is long and slender in a narrow russeted cavity of medium depth.
 The flesh is cream-cloured, crisp, and juicy.
