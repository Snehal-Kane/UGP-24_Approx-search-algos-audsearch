The Laurel Beach Casino is a historic meeting and entertainment facility at 102 6th Avenue in Milford, Connecticut.
  Built in 1929, it is the last surviving building of its type in the city, whose coastal villages once featured seven of them.
  The building is a Colonial Revival structure, featuring a wraparound porch, auditorium with stage, and a bowling alley.
  It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 2018.
